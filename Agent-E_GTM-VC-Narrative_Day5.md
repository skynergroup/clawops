## 1) One-Page Positioning (Investor + Buyer)

### Positioning statement
**ClawOps ISO** is an AI-assisted compliance operations layer that helps lean teams produce ISO-ready evidence packs faster and with fewer coordination failures. In 5 days, we can prove the “system of record + workflow + narrative” loop: ingest artifacts, map to controls, assign gaps, track closure, and export an auditor-facing package.

### Problem
- ISO readiness is fragmented across docs, tickets, screenshots, policies, and tribal memory.
- Teams lose time in “where is the evidence?” and “is this control covered?” loops.
- Founders/compliance leads overpay in consultant time because evidence is not structured, traceable, or current.

### Why now
- Security requirements now block enterprise deals earlier in startup lifecycles.
- AI makes unstructured artifact extraction and draft control mapping economically viable.
- Buyers want speed to assurance outcomes, not another static checklist tool.

### Product wedge (what Day 5 proves)
1. **Evidence ingestion** (files/links/notes) into one workspace.
2. **Control mapping draft** against ISO controls with confidence tags.
3. **Gap workflow** (owner, due date, status) with action queue.
4. **Audit narrative export**: concise, traceable evidence pack by control.

### ICP + champion
- ICP: B2B SaaS, fintech-enabling, IT services, agencies (20–300 employees).
- Champion: Ops/Security lead, CTO, or founder owning compliance.
- Economic buyer: CEO/COO/CFO who cares about deal unblock + reduced consultant hours.

### Value proposition (Day-5 conservative)
- **Time-to-first-evidence-pack** reduced from weeks to days.
- **Coverage visibility**: known mapped/unmapped controls with explicit confidence.
- **Lower coordination drag**: clear owner + deadline on each compliance gap.

### Why we can win
- Workflow-first vs checklist-first.
- Traceability: every control statement points to specific artifacts.
- “AI with receipts”: drafts are always reviewable/editable, never black box.

### 12-month expansion vision (framed as roadmap, not Day-5 claim)
- Continuous evidence sync (cloud tools), recurring controls health score, multi-framework overlays (SOC2/ISO/NIST), broker integrations.

## 4) Demo Script (5–7 minutes)

### Demo objective
Show a credible end-to-end loop proving ClawOps ISO can turn messy compliance inputs into a structured, auditable output quickly.

### Setup (15–20 sec)
“Today I’ll show how a small team goes from scattered compliance artifacts to an ISO-ready control narrative in minutes, with full traceability.”

### Step 1 — Ingest evidence (60–75 sec)
- Upload 3–5 sample artifacts (policy doc, access review screenshot, backup log, incident runbook).
- Show normalized metadata (source, date, owner, artifact type).

Talk track:
“Instead of hunting across drive folders and chat threads, we ingest evidence into one place with metadata so it can be referenced repeatedly.”

### Step 2 — Draft control mapping (75–90 sec)
- Open control list in scope.
- Show AI-suggested mapping with confidence labels.
- Click one mapping to show exact cited artifact snippet.

Talk track:
“We don’t ask users to trust a black box. Every suggested mapping links back to evidence, and confidence makes review workload explicit.”

### Step 3 — Surface and assign gaps (60–75 sec)
- Filter unmapped/low-confidence controls.
- Create gap tasks with owner + due date.
- Move one task status to “in progress” or “done”.

Talk track:
“Compliance failures are usually operational failures. This turns missing controls into accountable work items.”

### Step 4 — Export auditor-facing narrative (60–75 sec)
- Generate export for selected controls.
- Show concise control statement + linked evidence + gap status.

Talk track:
“This is the key outcome: a package an auditor or customer security reviewer can follow, without another week of manual stitching.”

### Close (30–45 sec)
“In five days, what we can prove is speed, traceability, and workflow clarity—not full automation. The next phase is depth: broader integrations, stronger confidence models, and multi-framework coverage.”

### Demo-safe claims checklist
Only say these if true in live environment:
- “This data is live from today’s workspace.”
- “Mappings are reviewable and editable.”
- “Each control claim has linked evidence.”
- “This export was generated during the demo.”

Avoid saying by Day 5:
- “Fully automated ISO certification.”
- “Zero human review needed.”
- “Enterprise-scale coverage proven.”

## 5) Risk / Defensibility Framing (Investor-ready)

### Key risks (and honest framing)
1. **Mapping accuracy risk**
   - Risk: false confidence or noisy suggestions.
   - Mitigation: confidence thresholds, mandatory reviewer approval, override analytics.

2. **Adoption risk (process change)**
   - Risk: teams keep old spreadsheet habits.
   - Mitigation: low-friction ingest, immediate export value, owner-based workflows.

3. **Integration/data quality risk**
   - Risk: poor upstream artifacts reduce output quality.
   - Mitigation: ingestion standards, artifact health checks, source completeness score.

4. **Crowded market narrative risk**
   - Risk: perceived as another “AI compliance copilot.”
   - Mitigation: lead with operational outcomes and audit-ready traceability, not generic AI chat.

### Defensibility pillars (credible, not hype)
1. **Evidence-Control Graph**
   - Structured linkage between artifacts, controls, owners, and decisions builds cumulative product intelligence.

2. **Human-in-the-loop feedback data**
   - Reviewer edits/approvals create proprietary tuning loops per control context.

3. **Workflow entrenchment**
   - Once teams run gap ownership and export cycles in-platform, switching costs rise.

4. **Outcome dataset**
   - Over time: benchmarks on time-to-readiness, mapping confidence, and remediation velocity.

### Investor language to use
- “We are proving the compliance operations loop, not claiming autonomous certification.”
- “Our moat compounds from structured evidence relationships and reviewer feedback.”
- “Day-5 output demonstrates feasibility; next milestones validate repeatability across accounts.”
